29 May 1798
Petition of Colon Krückeberg No. 10 (Document No. 1)
Krückeberg submits a request to expand his farmyard and garden. The petition is likely composed by or with the assistance of the Amt (administrative office).
30 May 1798
Document Nr. 1 marked "praes."
The document is formally presented to the Cammer. Marks the start of formal processing.
14 June 1798
Document Nr. 1 marked "B."
Berichtet – recorded in the Cammer’s registry under Cam: Nr. 662.
May–June 1798
Forstamt assessment by Officer Maas
Report concludes the plot can be allocated “without any disadvantage.” Tree replacement conditions included.
18 February 1799
Administrative marginal note
Cammer sends matter to the Amt “brevi manu” — informally — for further report.
27 February 1799
Report from Bückeburg Amt (Document Index No. 2)
Amt responds to the Cammer with updates or confirmations.
11 March 1800
Draft Chamber Instruction (Document Index No. 3)
Cammer instructs the Amt to proceed with further administrative action.
18 October 1800
Follow-up Report by Amt (Document Index No. 4)
Ongoing status report submitted by the Amt.
Undated (ca. Oct 1800)
Report by Lieutenant Funck (Document Index No. 4½)
Surveyor verifies boundaries and dimensions; map prepared.
27 October 1800
Draft Instructions (Document Index No. 5)
Preparation for purchase and coordination with Cammer Treasurer Barkmann.
1 December 1800
Draft Purchase Letter (Kaufbrief) (Document Index No. 6)
Draft of the official contract for Krückeberg’s plot expansion. Final stage of the case.
