re: no. D: 322. supra scripta                 5
Draft of the Chamber: Rescripts
to the officials at Bückeburg

              Very Gracious Lords,
              In reply to your report
              of the 18th of this month concerning the
              tenant farmer Krückeberg, No. 10 at Beren-
              busch, for the purpose of enlarging
              his farmyard and garden by
              19 rods 44 feet and
              1 rod 90 feet of allocated
              plots, and concerning the ground rent
              (Rottzins) on these, payable for the first time
              at Michaelmas of this year
              and thereafter annually in the amount of 2 Mariengroschen
              1 Pfennig, we hereby inform you without reservation that we
              have approved the allocation of these plots under

Cap: per Bolte.

              the conditions communicated to you
              in Our Rescript of
              11 March 1799, as well as the
              stated rate of the ground rent.
              You are therefore to ensure that
              the tenant farmer Krückeberg
              fulfills exactly the conditions
              that you have no doubt made known to him,
              and that he now pay the stipulated purchase price
              of 2 Mariengroschen 4 Pfennig per 4 rods,
              amounting in total
              to 14 Reichsthaler
              8 Mariengroschen 1 Pfennig,
              into Our Chamber treasury.
              We remain,
              Bückeburg, 27 October 1800
              For the Count

                       Mkaas
                       Wint
Mgr Danckwerk
....

[NOTE]
====
*Stirneichen* — The term appears here after “Nr: 10” in reference to Colon Krückeberg’s property.  He lived in
Berenbusch not "Stirneichen", which oes not correspond to any known village (*Ort*) in the region per *Meyers Orts-
und Verkehrslexikon* (1912). It may represent a misrecorded place name or possibly a *Flurname* (traditional field
name) used locally in the late 18th–early 19th century.  Its exact reference remains uncertain.
====
